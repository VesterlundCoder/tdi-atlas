## Key Person — Isaac Argyros

**Isaac Argyros** (c. 1305 – c. 1375) is the **primary mathematical interest** in this manuscript.

| Fact | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Dates | c. 1305–c. 1375 (Constantinople) |
| Profession | Monk, mathematician, astronomer, philosopher |
| Mathematical works | *Treatise on the quadratic irrational* (De irrationalibus); revision of Ptolemy's *Handy Tables*; treatises on the astrolabe; correction of the Julian calendar |
| Relation to Vat.gr.1777 | Scholia / commentary on Aristotle's *De Interpretatione* (ff. 79r–v) |
| Significance | Last major Byzantine mathematician before the fall of Constantinople; student of Nikephoros Gregoras |
| Other MSS | Vat.gr.184, Par.gr.2390, Marc.gr.323 |

Argyros represents the **late Byzantine synthesis of Aristotelian logic and mathematical reasoning** — directly relevant to this research project.

---

## Key Person — Gennadius Scholarius

**Gennadius Scholarius** (c. 1405 – c. 1472/3), first Patriarch of Constantinople under Ottoman rule. His *Expositio* on Aristotle's *De Interpretatione* occupies the largest portion of this manuscript (ff. 79v–108v). He was a student of the Aristotelian tradition and engaged with both Western scholasticism and Byzantine Platonic/Aristotelian synthesis.
